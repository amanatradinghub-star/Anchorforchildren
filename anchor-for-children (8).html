Who we are

Rooted in community.
Driven by purpose.

Anchor for Children (AFC) is a community-based, non-profit organization committed to advancing the protection, wellbeing, and development of vulnerable children and families in underserved communities across Kenya. AFC operates at the intersection of child protection, education, health, and community resilience — applying a rights-based, participatory approach.

Vision

A society where all children live in safety, dignity, and have equitable access to opportunities for growth and development.

Mission

To design and implement sustainable, community-led interventions that protect children, strengthen families, and improve access to essential services including education, health, and psychosocial support.

Strategic objectives

01Strengthen community-based child protection systems
02Improve access to quality, inclusive education for vulnerable children
03Enhance psychosocial wellbeing and resilience among children and youth
04Promote child health, nutrition, and HIV/AIDS support
05Build family and community protective capacity
06Advance gender equality and protection of the girl child
Our team

People at the heart of our work

Our field staff are the backbone of AFC — trusted, trained, and deeply embedded in the communities they serve across Kenya's arid and semi-arid regions.

Who our team are

AFC staff are drawn from the communities they serve — bringing deep local knowledge, cultural competency, and lived experience to every interaction. Many are survivors and advocates themselves.

Training & capacity

All staff are trained in child protection standards, psychosocial first aid, case management, and safeguarding. Regular capacity-building ensures our team delivers evidence-informed, rights-based programming.

Gender & inclusion

AFC prioritizes gender-balanced staffing and inclusion of persons from marginalized backgrounds — reflecting the diversity of the communities we serve and the children we protect.

Field presence

AFC teams operate across Marsabit and Isiolo Counties, with reach into remote pastoralist communities, schools, health facilities, and community gathering spaces.

How we work

Our approach & methodology

AFC adopts a rights-based, participatory, and community-driven approach — ensuring community ownership, sustainability, and alignment with international child protection standards.

Rights-based programming

Alignment with the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child and national child protection frameworks.

Community ownership

Communities are central actors — not beneficiaries — in designing, implementing, and sustaining every intervention.

Safeguarding & accountability

Zero-tolerance for abuse. Robust safeguarding systems and transparent accountability to affected populations.

Evidence-informed MEL

Results-based management with regular monitoring, data-driven decisions, and transparent reporting.

Gender & inclusion

Cross-cutting integration of gender equality and protection needs of the girl child across all programs.
